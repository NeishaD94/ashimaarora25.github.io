Northern Lights in Iceland

The Aurora Borealis takes its name from the Roman goddess of dawn, Aurora, and the Greek name for the north wind, Boreax. From September to April, Iceland is a hotspot for this magnificient lightshow.
The Aurora boralis, referred to as the Northern Lights is a natural phenomenon created when particles emitted by the sun interact with the atmosphere in the Earth's magnetic field. This releases energy, causing peculiar luminous green streaks across the skies.

When making a trip to Iceland, it is hard to pay special attention to the country's namesake-namely, its 4,500 square miles of glacier.
Ice climbing on the glaciers is practiced year around. Ice climbing & hiking on glaciers and takes place mainly on the Sólheimajökull and Svínafellsjökull glaciers in the south of Iceland, to which day trips are offered from Reykjavík (and Skaftafell).
